About

I design systems that explore how intelligent systems interact with economic infrastructure in real-world settings. My work focuses on how intelligent systems act, interact, and can be governed—combining empirical measurement, causal inference, and system design. Broadly, I’m interested in designing systems that move beyond static models toward adaptive, self-correcting infrastructure.

I’m particularly interested in building systems that are verifiable, adaptive, and capable of supporting decentralized decision-making in real-world environments.

Now

Studying how interaction structure shapes agent behavior and system-level outcomes, through experimental and empirical approaches.
