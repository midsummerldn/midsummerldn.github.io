About

I design systems at the intersection of AI agents, economic infrastructure, and decentralized systems. My work focuses on how intelligent systems act, interact, and can be governed—combining empirical measurement, causal inference, and system design. Broadly, I’m interested in designing systems that move beyond static models toward adaptive, self-correcting infrastructure.

I’m particularly interested in building systems that are verifiable, adaptive, and capable of supporting decentralized decision-making in real-world environments.

What am I upto?

Running large-scale LLM interaction experiments to study how intervention structure shapes agent behavior and system outcomes.
